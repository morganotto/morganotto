Interior Design for the Low Income Home



In order to address the issue of inaccessibility of interior design to low income individuals, I built this project to raise awareness to the possibility of upcycling and buying used furniture.

This project responds to stereotypes about buying used furniture, as well as teaching the audience methods of furniture upcycling.

The first element of this project is a 126 page publication that describes the value in thrift furniture, as well as methods of upcycling. The design approach is catered towards young adults, more specifically individuals who are interested in a contemporary aesthetic but unable to afford brand new furniture.

9inx13.5in
2021

programs used: adobe illustrator, indesign, photoshop

The second phase of this project includes a typographic application designed to exist within thrift stores.

This element begins with a laser-cut tag applied to furniture that includes a QR code which will redirect the scanner to an instagram-like story. This story will discuss methods of upcycling to encourage the viewer to consider upcycling as a method of affordable interior design. This element addresses ideas of sustainable shopping as well as accessibility.

adobe illustrator, figma, laser cut
